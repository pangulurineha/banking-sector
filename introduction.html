INTRODUCTION

Banks play a vital role in the financial system, providing individuals and businesses with a secure place to store money, process transactions, and access various financial services. With the advancement of technology, banking has become more convenient through online banking, mobile applications, and ATMs. However, as banking services expand, security risks have also increased. Fraudulent activities such as phishing, identity theft, ATM fraud, and online scams have led to significant financial losses for customers. Many individuals, particularly those in rural areas, lack awareness of these risks and do not know how to protect themselves from banking fraud. This lack of knowledge makes them vulnerable to scams, putting their hard-earned money at risk.

Banking fraud has become a major concern worldwide, affecting both customers and financial institutions. While banks implement various security measures to protect customers, criminals continuously develop new techniques to bypass these safeguards. Cybercriminals use tactics such as fake emails, malicious links, and social engineering to deceive people into revealing their confidential banking information. Additionally, traditional banking frauds, such as counterfeit currency and forged documents, continue to pose risks. With the increasing number of fraud cases, it has become necessary to educate people about banking security and find effective ways to make banking safer.

This project, "Ideas for Safety in the Banking Sector," aims to understand the level of awareness people have about banking fraud, identify the most common threats, and explore ways to improve security measures. The primary objectives of this study are to assess how well individuals understand banking security, document the types of fraud they have encountered, collect ideas on how banking safety can be improved, and suggest practical steps to enhance banking security. By analysing customer experiences and expert opinions, this project provides insights into the challenges of banking security and proposes recommendations for both customers and financial institutions.

To conduct this research, a survey was carried out with 30 participants from different regions within the Nellore district. These participants included both urban and rural residents to ensure a diverse understanding of banking safety across different communities. The survey focused on gathering information about their experiences with banking fraud, their awareness of online and offline security risks, and their opinions on improving banking safety. In addition to the survey, we visited several banks and spoke directly with customers and bank employees. These conversations provided firsthand insights into real-life fraud cases, the preventive measures banks currently have in place, and the challenges faced in maintaining security.

Our field visits extended beyond urban areas to nearby villages, where people primarily use banks for savings and basic transactions. Many rural customers rely on banking services but lack knowledge about digital threats and security practices. While urban customers who use digital banking services are somewhat familiar with the risks associated with online fraud, many rural customers remain unaware of basic safety measures such as setting strong passwords, avoiding sharing OTPs, and recognizing fraudulent messages. Our findings indicate that despite efforts by banks to implement security measures such as OTP verification, SMS alerts, and multi-factor authentication, many customers still fall victim to fraud due to a lack of awareness and negligence.

Through this project, we aim to contribute to banking safety by raising awareness, identifying key security concerns, and suggesting practical solutions to enhance financial security. By implementing stronger security measures and educating customers about banking fraud, we can create a safer banking environment for everyone.
